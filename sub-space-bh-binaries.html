Binaries

 
click here

Many of the stars in the night sky are binaries. Binary means two pieces or parts. It this case, binaries are two stars that are locked into orbit around each other.

The brightest star in the night sky, Sirius, is part of a binary. Alpha Centauri, one of the closest stars to Earth, is also part of a binary.

Sometimes we find a star and a black hole orbiting each other. Cygnus X-1 is the best known example of a black hole and a star orbiting each other.

The Cygnus X-1 black hole is sucking gas from the star beside it. As the gas falls towards the black hole, the gas gets so hot that X-rays are produced.

We can see these X-rays with satellites, and make images of what it might look like if we could see it up close. It would be scary but very beautiful!
